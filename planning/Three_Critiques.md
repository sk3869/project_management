Pass 1 — Structural Pass (Is the shape correct?)
This pass checks form, not content. You ignore wording, style, and details.

You ask:

Does it follow the required schema or format?

Are all required sections present?

Is anything missing?

Is anything in the wrong place?

Does the order make sense?

Is the length roughly correct?

You do not fix sentences here.
You only fix structure.

This prevents you from polishing something that is fundamentally wrong.

Pass 2 — Clarity Pass (Does it communicate clearly?)
Now you check meaning and readability.

You ask:

Is every sentence clear?

Does the logic flow?

Are there confusing phrases?

Does the reader understand the purpose?

Is anything vague, redundant, or drifting?

You rewrite sentences, tighten language, and remove confusion.

You do not add new ideas here — only clarify what’s already there.

Pass 3 — Quality Pass (Is it polished and correct?)
This is the final refinement.

You ask:

Are the facts correct?

Are the terms consistent?

Are the examples appropriate?

Are there grammar or punctuation issues?

Does the tone match the assignment?

Does it feel finished?

This is where you polish, not rethink.
